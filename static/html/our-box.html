CHIYOMUSUBI SAKE BREWERY

Tottori Prefecture

Established in 1865, Chiyomusubi (which means eternally ties with people) brewery is located in Tottori prefecture and focuses on Sake made from Goriki -strong power- rice. Indeed, Sake made from Goriki rice ages very well, has a unique character with a rich taste and gentle aroma. The rice is only cultivated in Tottori prefecture and to preserve its purity, only registered farmers can cultivate it. Surrounded by great environment, they provide happiness and eternal ties with people through Sake.




FUKUMITSUYA SAKE BREWERY

Ishikawa Prefecture

Premium Sakes fall into two categories: Junmai-shu and Honjozo-shu. They are both made from rice, water, yeast, and koji however Honjozo-shu contains a small amount of distilled alcohol, Junmai-shu does’nt. Fukumitsuya is classified as a “Junmai-Gura”, a Sake Brewery which specializes in Junmai-shu. Since 1625, this “Junmai-Gura” based in Kanazawa, Ishikawa, uses natural mineral water, superior quality of Sake rice, and its traditional brewing skills to create the highest quality of Junmai Sake.



YAMAMOTO HONKE


Kyoto Prefecture

Yamamoto Honke started brewing in the beginning of the Edo period around 1677, in Fushimi in Kyoto prefecture, ancient capital of Japan. During the famous Battle of Toba-Fushimi in 1868, the brewery was severely damaged. However it raised from the ashes and to this day produces a Sake known for its charm, elegance and softness. The brewery sources its water from Shiragiku -water of white chrysanthemum- a water spring located in the surrounding mountains.
